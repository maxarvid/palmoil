Palm company throws temper tantrum in Liberia

BY Max Anderson

Published December 00, 2018

What does it take for a powerful company to show its true colors? For Golden Veroleum Liberia Inc., it began with a letter. Golden Veroleum is a palm oil company, with a large concession on the Liberian coast since 2010. This summer it ended its membership in the Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil, an industry-led multi-stakeholder group which aims to ensure the sustainability of palm oil, because of a complaint filed against the company in 2012.

Liberian Palm Oil Concessions

There are eight palm oil concessions in Liberia. Golden Veroleum's concessions are located in Sinoe and Grand Kru Counties.

SOURCE: Global Forest Watch, UN OCHA

The world, and more importantly the consumer, is gradually waking up to the the harms of palm oil. Innocuous sounding vegetable oil packs a slew of social and environmental harms , to the point that supermarkets go to great lengths to ban the sale of products containing palm oil in their stores.

A complicated context

Palm oil grows best in recently deforested areas near the equator. Concessions have in the past been accompanied by accusations of land-grabbing, with indigenous populations particularly at risk. Only natural then that the industry has bounded together and formed the Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil to address and mitigate some of these social harms (considering how much more difficult it would be to respond to the environmental ones).

Liberian officials were possibly aware of this when they invited Golden Veroleum to explore new concessions in the south of the country.
It would be simplistic to make claims that concessions were granted for the enrichment of the few. Liberia is a country that has, not too long ago, experienced several civil wars, an Ebola epidemic and as a result has a very limited commodity trade.

SOURCE: Global Forest Watch, World Bank

So who produces the most palm oil?

Make no mistake, Indonesia is still the largest producer of palm oil.
They are far ahead in terms of area of palm oil concessions.

But if we consider portion of available farm land, Liberia has dedicated more land for palm oil production.
We can thus deduce the importance and opportunity the Liberian government saw in palm oil.

Indonesia, Malaysia, Liberia, Cameroon, and the Republic of Congo are some of the palm oil exporting countrties in the world.

These countries all lie in close proximity to the equator.
